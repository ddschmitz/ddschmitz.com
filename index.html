Darrin Schmitz



Hello! My name is Darrin Schmitz. I am currently a graduate student at Dakota State University obtaining a master’s degree Applied Computer Science. I am also currently an adjunct instructor for DSU, teaching CSC-150. I have had various paid internships with organizations such as Daktronics, Citibank, and Los Alamos National Laboratory. If you would like to learn more about me and the things I've participated in and worked on please feel free to check them out!

About Me

Backstory

I was born and raised on a farm outside of Brewster, Minnesota. Growing up I never really had much of an interest in the technical aspects of computers. A majority of the time I spent using them was either playing games or watching videos. Looking back, I wish I would have had the foresight to start messing around with some coding tutorials. Or since I was into playing games on my computer, maybe start taking an interest in computer hardware, just as a means to get my feet in the water. A lot of my classmates and friends who I consider extremely knowledgeable and techy, all seemed to have started back when they were in high school. But hindsight is always 20/20 and luckily now I have the drive and passion to get better at a lot of computer science topics. Since I’ve started college I’ve developed the drive to better myself as a programmer and have a desire to never stop learning as much as I can.

Interests

Through my undergraduate studies I noticed a change in my interests. I initially chose to get my degree in computer and network security because I had an interest in the security field. This degree at Dakota State was more or less a computer science degree but with five or so security related classes. I noticed throughout my degree, I seemed to enjoy and learn a lot more from my computer science classes. My programming languages and language processing classes actually turned out to be two of the best classes I took. Because of this new appreciation for the computer science field, I decided to do my master’s degree at Dakota State in applied computer science rather than information security.
