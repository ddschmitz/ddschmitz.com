B.S. in Computer and Network Security


Dakota State University is an absolutely amazing institution. I enjoyed my time there tremendously while at the same time building a great amount of respect for the faculty. While there I received my bachelor’s in Computer and Network Security. You can think of this degree as a computer science degree with a big emphasis on security. I took a lot of the classes that a comp. sci. student would take such data structures, object oriented programming, and language processing. But in addition to those, I also took classes focused more on security such as offensive and defensive network security, web software security, and malware analysis. (Pertaining to the link above; the program for B.S. in Computer and Network Security changed to Cyber Ops in 2014)

The MSACS degree is essentially just a computer science degree, however they had to add the applied part to it in order to get it accepted by the South Dakota Board of Reagents. Digi-Key Collegiate Computing Competition (DKC3)

October 2015


During the 2015 school year, Dakota State University was invited for the first time to compete in the Digi-Key Collegiate Computing Competition. Representing DSU was two teams of four, one of which I was a member of. The competition consisted of three sections: short coding problems, word problems, and long coding problems. Teams were given one computer with various software installed to complete the challenges. Our team utilized Java and Python to complete the problems.

Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition (CCDC)

2014 – 2015 School Year


CCDC is a defensive security competition comprised of teams from various colleges throughout the nation. Teams are given identical networks and tasked with their defense. The intent of these networks is to simulate a real business network. As you attempt to secure your network, a group of security professionals actively attempts to breach your network.
