Rear Differential Gears

The first thing we ever had to repair on my car was actually something we discovered immediately after I purchased it. On the drive home from the dealership my brother noticed that there was a very faint rattle or grinding feeling when driving down the road. There wasn't an audible sound associated with it but you could definitely feel it when driving. Luckily my brother is a great mechanic and assumed there was something wrong with the rear differential. So as much as it saddened me, I had to keep my new car in the shed until the new parts came and we could get around to fixing it. But they did eventually come and below are some pictures of the process!

Turns out my brothers assumption was correct! The bearing on the left is the bad one that was causing all the troubles. The one on the right is one of the new ones that we put in.

That long shaft next to the wooden blocks is actually one half of the independent rear suspension. There are two of them and one end comes out of the differential and the other end connects to the wheels. Since they are separate they can move up and down independently. On most cars they just have one long solid axle that extends from one tire to the other. This was my first time seeing this and I thought it was very interesting!
